The development economists should stop acting as categorical advocates (or detractors) for specific approaches to development. They should instead be diagnosticians helping policy-makers to calibrate the existing models according to given constraints.

Growth Diagnostic Framework

Hausmann, Rodrik, and Velasco (2008)

What is Growth Diagnostic Framework?

Growth diagnostics is based on the idea that not all constraints bind equally, and that a sensible and practical strategy consists of identifying the most serious constraint(s) at work.

IDENTIFY THE PROBLEM

This is the first obvious step to know the target problem statement.

About 92 percent of garment firms in India have fewer than eight employees as shown by a combined dataset (for formal-sector firms from the Annual Survey of Industries and informal-sector firms from the National Sample Survey)

IDENTIFY THE CONSTRAINT

Find the right obstacle which inhibits the economy from being better off. Find whether the constraint is binding or not.

The stringent labour laws, more specifically Chapter VB of the Industrial Disputes Act (IDA) is the constraint that inhibits the small scale garment from upscaling themselves

Chapter V-B of IDA prohibits firms with more than 100 workers to lay off any worker without the prior permission from the government.

IS THE CONSTRAINT BINDING?

Identify the signals to know whether the constraint is binding or not.

As per the evidence, the constraint of Chapter V-B of IDA does not bind. Labour law cannot discourage an eight-employee firm from expanding to an 80-employee firm since Chapter VB of the IDA does not kick in until the firm reaches the size of 100 employees.

Diagnostic process

  • Identify the constraint

    The constraint represents the stimulus that results in the present condition of the economy.

  • Identify the signal

    What signal would the economy emit if the hypothesized constraint were indeed the binding. For example, in an economy that is constrained by the supply of capital, the cost of capital would be inversely related to investment.

  • Identify the remedy

    The second step in growth diagnostics is to identify remedies for relaxing the constraint and take cognizance of second-best complications
